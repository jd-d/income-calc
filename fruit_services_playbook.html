Part C — Detailed Service Chapters

1) Representation Retainers

Plain description. A steady monthly arrangement where you act as the client’s commercial point person. You keep relationships warm with suppliers or buyers, prepare/confirm offers and purchase orders, keep tabs on documents and arrivals, and flag problems early. Goal: fewer surprises, quicker decisions, and calmer weeks.

Who hires & when it’s a fit.
Importer/retailer → upstream coordination. Grower/exporter → downstream contact & follow-through. Best for clients who need reliable week-to-week support but not a full-time hire.

Effort rhythm.
Steady 9–5 pattern with mild peaks at season change or promo weeks. Follow-ups: light to moderate.

Options & tiers

TierInclusionsTypical HoursNotes
Lite 1 pipeline call/week; up to 5 active offers; simple ETA nudges; claims triage up to 2/month ~5 hrs/week Scope fence for extra calls/claims
Standard Lite + mid-week check-ins; simple buyer/supplier meetings; weekly summary note; up to 10 active offers; claims triage up to 4/month ~8 hrs/week Good baseline for most clients
Plus Standard + monthly on-site day or roadshow block + pre-season planning call; up to 15 active offers; claims triage up to 6/month ~12 hrs/week + 1 on-site day/mo Convert extra on-sites to add-ons

2) Operations Advisory (projects / day-rate)

Plain description. Short, focused projects that fix a process or reach a specific outcome—e.g., faster intake flow, clearer specs, fewer claims, or a smoother season kick-off. Work is scoped in days so everyone knows what gets done and when.

Who hires & fit.
Any client with a known pain point: “we miss windows,” “claims drag on,” “specs keep shifting.”

Effort rhythm.
Bursty during workshops and trials; calmer during follow-ups. Easy to schedule around other work.

3) QC Inspections (per-visit)

Plain description. On-site checks at a warehouse, market, or port to see if arrivals match expectations. You sample, take clear photos, note defects, and send a same-day report so the client can accept, reroute, or claim with confidence. Priced per visit, with simple travel rules.

Who hires & fit.
Any client receiving fruit who needs quick, trustworthy eyes on the ground—especially in peaks.

Effort rhythm.
Bursty during season peaks; calm otherwise. Field time + short desk time.

4) Claims Handling & Dispute Support (add-on)

Plain description. Structured follow-through to resolve quality/shortage disputes: build an evidence pack, contact counterparties, and drive to a fair outcome on time.

Scope & steps

  2. Counterparty contact: share evidence; agree target timeline.
  3. Negotiation: options (credit, rework, disposal) with decision owner.

Part D — Scoping & Pricing Playbook

1) Universal scoping checklist

QuestionWhy it matters
What is the concrete outcome?Prevents scope creep; defines done.
Who is the decision owner?Unblocks delays; sets escalation path.
What is the time window?Aligns availability & seasonality.
Volumes / lots / lanes?Right-sizes hours, travel, and KPIs.
Specs & prior issues?Surfaces risks early; guides checks.
Communication channel?Speeds decisions; keeps record.

2) Scope fence recipes

Retainer: weekly rhythm, hour bands, claims cap, “quiet if no news.” Extras → add-ons.

Per-day: fixed outcomes per day, capped days, change-note for extras.

Per-visit: visits/lot caps, report turnaround, cluster rules, travel zones.

Fixed package: named deliverables, timeline, explicit exclusions.

3) KPIs you can actually observe

Prefer counts and turnaround times you can see weekly. Keep dashboards simple.

4) Seasonality & surge rules

  • Pre-agree surge: after N urgent calls/claims, bill day-rate add-on.
  • Define blackout dates and travel day caps per month.

5) Travel rules

  • Cluster visits geographically; group buyer meetings into a roadshow day.
  • Define local vs regional zones up front.

6) Pricing fences & comfort buffer

  • Min: acceptable floor if scope is clean and cadence is steady.
  • Target: preferred price that reflects your true effort.
  • Stretch: covers risk, rush, or complexity.

7) Costs, tax, and take-home realism

Reserve a % for taxes/insurance; reflect travel and material costs; sanity-check weekly hours against your desired schedule.

Part G — Light Policies & Terms

  • Cancellation: ≥ 3 business days: no fee; < 3 days: 50% of first day/visit; same-day/no-show: 100% of first day/visit.
  • Rush / out-of-hours: +25% evenings/weekends; +40% public holidays.
  • Travel: Zones as per Part D; travel day surcharge or €0.45/km beyond Local.
  • Materials: At cost with prior approval.
  • Confidentiality: Mutual NDA language available on request.
  • Liability cap: Capped at fees paid for the specific engagement.
  • Payment terms: 14 days net; retainers monthly in advance.

I) Glossary (plain-English)

  • 9–5 / steady: Work that fits normal office hours without frequent emergencies.
  • Add-on: Extra work not in the main scope, billed separately.
  • Blackout dates: Dates you don’t take work or travel.
  • Change note: A short written agreement to expand scope or days and adjust price.
  • Claim: A complaint about quality/quantity that may lead to credit or other remedy.
  • Claim cycle time: Days from raising a claim to resolving it.
  • Cluster / roadshow: Planning several site or buyer visits in one trip/day.
  • Comms channel: Agreed place for updates (email, WhatsApp, etc.).
  • Decision owner: The person who makes the final call on price, POs, or disputes.
  • Dispute cycle: Days from disagreement to a signed solution.
  • ETA: Estimated time of arrival.
  • Evidence pack: Photos, measurements, and documents used to support a claim.
  • First-time-right: Doing a task correctly without needing rework.
  • Follow-up load: The amount of chasing and checking needed after the main task.
  • Fences (pricing): Min / Target / Stretch price points you present to a client.
  • KPI: Key performance indicator — a simple number that shows performance.
  • Lane: A route/country pair or corridor for shipping/selling.
  • Lot: A batch of fruit with the same identity shipped/received together.
  • On-site: Work done at a physical location like a warehouse or market.
  • Pass/fail grid (photo-led): A simple picture-based rule for accept or reject.
  • PO (Purchase Order): The client’s order document confirming a buy.
  • RACI: A table that says who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed.
  • Retainer: A monthly fee for ongoing support/service.
  • Seasonality: Regular peaks/slumps over the year tied to harvest or demand.
  • Scope creep: The work slowly expands beyond what was agreed.
  • Simple dashboard: A basic sheet or table with just the few KPIs that matter.
  • SOP: Standard operating procedure — a simple “how we do it” guide.
  • Spec: Specification — what “good” looks like for the fruit or packaging.
  • Stretch price: A higher price that covers extra risk, speed, or complexity.
  • Surge: A short period when workload spikes, often in season peaks.
  • TAT: Turnaround time — how quickly you respond or deliver.
  • Travel day surcharge: A fee when a whole/half day is consumed by travel.
  • Triaging (claims): Sorting a new issue so it goes to the right path fast.
  • Weekly pipeline: The current list of offers, POs, and shipments in motion.
